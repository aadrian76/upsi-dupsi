## 🏗️ Interns

You'll follow a **guided approach**, with topics and exercises split into **two phases**, each building on the last. So don't jump ahead—**you'll just make things harder for yourself later**.

1. **Core Concepts**: Learn and apply the basics
2. **Deepen Understanding**: Cover additional topics and finalize deliverables

You will have **two checkpoints**:

- **Midweek Checkpoint**. To fix mistakes early
- **Final Checkpoint**. To finalize and submit everything

</div>

- [PaaS](https://dev.azure.com/Cloud-Application-Security/Training%20Path/_wiki/wikis/Training%20Path/113/%E2%98%81%EF%B8%8F-Cloud-Security?anchor=%E2%9A%99%EF%B8%8F-platform-as-a-service-(paas))
- [SaaS](https://dev.azure.com/Cloud-Application-Security/Training%20Path/_wiki/wikis/Training%20Path/113/%E2%98%81%EF%B8%8F-Cloud-Security?anchor=%F0%9F%8E%89-software-as-a-service-(saas))

If anything is unclear, remember, **Google is your friend**. If it's still unclear, ask your trainer. They're there to help you learn, not just to review your work.

### 🛠 2. Practical Exercises

Now, apply what you just learned through these two tasks. These **must be completed before the Midweek Checkpoint**.

The tasks **must** be completed in order. Don't skip ahead. But most importantly, **DO NOT HALF-ASS them**—you'll only make more work for yourself later.

#### 📝 Task 1: Client Email

Write an email explaining OWASP Top 10 to a (simulated) client in a clear, structured, and professional way.

**What to include:**

- What is OWASP Top 10?
- How does it compare to OWASP ASVS?
- Why does this matter for their company?
- A real-world breach example tied to OWASP Top 10

**Requirements:**

- Keep it professional—concise, structured, and straight to the point
- **Time limit**: 1.5 hours max—if you spend longer, you're overthinking it

<div style="background:#F3E8FF; padding:15px; margin-bottom:15px; border-left:10px solid #7C3AED; border-radius:6px; color:#1F1B2D;">
    <b>💡 Tip.</b> Great emails take time. They need to be clear and to the point—too long and no one reads them, too vague and they're useless. Keep that balance, is hard...
</div>

#### 📝 Task 2: First Half of a PPT

Create a structured presentation explaining **SaaS, IaaS, and PaaS** with clear visuals and real-world examples.

This PPT is split into two parts, so we can do a checkpoint review before you complete it. Your task is to create only the **first half** of the PPT presentation (**3 content slides max**), not the full thing.

**What to include:** *(topics for the full PPT, you decide what to include in the first half)*

- Definition of SaaS, IaaS, and PaaS; hat they are and how they work
- Real-world examples of each service model
- Comparison of strengths and weaknesses. Why'd a company would choose one over another?
- Diagram or comparison table

**Requirements:**

- Add visual elements—NO text dumps
- Slides must be self-explanatory—anyone should understand them without you presenting. This doesn't mean you should CRAM everything into one slide, NOPE
- **Max 3 content slides**—cover, index, and transition slides do not count

The **Midweek Checkpoint** will review formatting, structure, and clarity before you complete the second half

<div style="background:#F3E8FF; padding:15px; margin-bottom:15px; border-left:10px solid #7C3AED; border-radius:6px; color:#1F1B2D;">
    <b>⚠️ If your slides look like they were made by a blind monkey, expect pain.</b> In Accenture, we don't do "ugly" slides. So, take your time, don't rush it, and make sure to review other PPTs to get an idea of what's expected
</div>

### 🔎 Midweek Checkpoint

This is your chance to fix mistakes early before finalizing your work. Yor trainer will review:

- **Client Email**. Checks clarity, structure, and correctness. No fluff, no AI-generated nonsense
- **First Half of a PPT**. Ensures formatting, layout, and structure follows the CAS standards. If your slides are cluttered, unclear, or missing key points, you'll be redoing it
- **Understanding**. Expect questions to check if you actually get the first few topics

Use this opportunity to:

- Get feedback and avoid last-minute fixes
- Ask questions about any concepts from the first part—if something still doesn't make sense, **ask now**
- Fix formatting issues—because "good enough" won't cut it

If your email is vague or your slides are a mess, expect corrections. Better to fix them now than redo everything later, right?

### 📖 4. Learn Additional Concepts

You're **expected to apply what you learn**. If something in your deliverables looks off, **fix it before the checkpoint**—don't wait for someone to point it out.

### 🛠 5. Practical Exercises

Now, complete the final tasks. These **must be completed before the Final Checkpoint**.

#### 📝 Task 3: Complete the PPT

After the Midweek Checkpoint, you'll finish the second half of the presentation. This is your chance to apply the feedback you received and make sure the entire PPT is solid.

**What to include:** *(full PPT)*

- Definition of SaaS, IaaS, and PaaS—what they are and how they work
- Real-world examples of each service model
- Comparison of strengths and weaknesses. Why'd a company would choose one over another?
- Diagram or comparison table

**Requirements:**

- Add visual elements—NO text dumps
- Slides must be self-explanatory—anyone should understand them without you presenting. This doesn't mean you should CRAM everything into one slide, NOPE
- **Max 5 content slides total, not just the second half**—cover, index, and transition slides do not count

#### 📝 Task 4: WORD Report

Write a technical report explaining SAST, DAST, and SCA, **comparing their strengths, weaknesses, and** real-world **usage**. This is not just a list of definitions—you need to justify and analyze their use cases.

**What to include:**

- Overview of the document: what it covers and why it matters
- Clear explanations of SAST, DAST, and SCA; what they do and how they work
- 2-3 real-world tools for each—common tools, no obscure nonsense. Justify your selection: explain why these are industry-standard or widely used
- A conclusion that summarizes the key points, plus a comparison table to see differences

**Requirements:**

- At least one relevant diagram or example (architecture, workflow, NOT logos)
- **Max 5 content pages**—cover, index, and transition pages do not count

<div style="background:#F3E8FF; padding:15px; margin-bottom:15px; border-left:10px solid #7C3AED; border-radius:6px; color:#1F1B2D;">
    <b>📣 Remember.</b> Your Trainer is here to help you learn. If you're unsure about your PPT or report, ask for feedback before submission—not when it's too late
</div>

### 🔎 Self-Review Before Submission

If your final submission is full of avoidable mistakes, **it's 100% on you**. Run through this checklist before submitting:

1. **Client Email**. Does it follow the format? Is it clear, structured, and concise?
2. **PPT**. Are the slides well-formatted, visually clear, and easy to understand?
3. **WORD Report**. Is it concise, well-organized, and actually makes sense?

If you can't answer "yes" to all, you're not done yet. **Sloppy work = redoing it**.

Take your time, review everything, and make sure it's solid before submission.

### 🔎 Final Checkpoint

Your trainer will review:

- **PPT**. Structure, content, formatting, and clarity
- **Word Report**. Completeness, correctness, and overall presentation
- **Understanding.** Expect questions to check if you actually get the rest of the topics

This is your **last chance to fix mistakes** before closing the week. If something is missing or unclear, **you will be expected to correct it**.
